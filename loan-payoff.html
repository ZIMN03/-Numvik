How to pay off debt faster

Even small additional payments each month can dramatically reduce both payoff time and total interest. On a $10,000 loan at 18% APR with a $300 minimum payment, adding just $100/month saves over $1,300 in interest and removes more than a year from your payoff timeline.

Two common strategies: the avalanche method targets your highest-rate debt first, minimizing total interest paid. The snowball method targets the smallest balance first, providing faster psychological wins to maintain momentum. Both work — the best one is whichever keeps you consistent.
