How compound interest works

Compound interest means you earn returns not just on your original principal, but also on the interest you've already accumulated. Over time, this creates an exponential growth curve rather than a straight line.

For example, $5,000 at 7% over 20 years:

  • Simple interest: $5,000 + $7,000 = $12,000
  • Compound interest (monthly): approximately $20,193 — nearly double

Adding regular monthly contributions amplifies this effect significantly. Time in the market is the single most powerful variable — starting ten years earlier will often matter more than doubling your contribution amount.
