How much do you need to retire?

A widely used benchmark is the 25x rule: accumulate 25 times your expected annual expenses. This is grounded in the 4% rule, which suggests a portfolio can sustain withdrawals of 4% per year indefinitely, based on historical market returns.

So if you want $4,000/month ($48,000/year) in retirement, you'd need approximately $1,200,000 saved. Social Security or other income sources can reduce this target.

Starting early is disproportionately powerful. A 25-year-old saving $300/month will frequently retire with more than a 35-year-old saving $600/month — because the extra decade of compounding makes up the difference and then some.
