How this calculator works

Enter your retirement goal — the total amount you want to have saved when you retire — along with your current age, target retirement age, and what you already have saved. The calculator figures out exactly how much you need to set aside each month to get there.

The math accounts for compound growth on your existing savings, so the more you already have, the less you need to save going forward. Your "Monthly Income at Retirement" is estimated using the 4% rule — a widely-used benchmark that says a portfolio can support annual withdrawals of 4% of its value without running out.

Starting early is disproportionately powerful. A 25-year-old saving $300/month will frequently retire with more than a 35-year-old saving $600/month — because the extra decade of compounding makes up the difference and then some.
