How lenders determine affordability

Most lenders use the 28/36 rule: your monthly housing payment should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income, and total debt payments (housing + all other debts) should not exceed 36% of gross monthly income.

This calculator uses the 28% front-end ratio to calculate the maximum monthly payment, then works backwards to the maximum home price given your down payment, interest rate, and loan term.

Keep in mind that lenders also weigh your credit score, employment history, and cash reserves. This calculator gives you a reliable estimate — a lender pre-approval will give you the exact number for your situation.
